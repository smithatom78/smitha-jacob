About Kerala

Kerala is indeed a remix of everything you could possibly think from the vibrant festivals, spice-scented markets, glinting lagoons, and waterways, to those pristine palm-fringed beaches.Hiking into the mountains, boating through the backwaters, cycling through the spice gardens and coconut plantations, tasting the delicious food, and exploring the beaches, of course, Kerala has it all waiting for you.

TOP DESTINATIONS

Athirappilly Water Falls

Athirappilly Water Falls is situated in Athirappilly Panchayat of Thrissur District in Kerala. It is the largest waterfall in Kerala, which stands tall at 80 feet.

Cherai Beach

Cherai Beach is a beach located in Cherai in the northern side of Vypin Island. The most visited beaches in the state, it is situated at around 25 km from downtown Kochi.

Silent Valley National Parks

It is located in the Nilgiri hills, has a core area of 89.52 km² and surrounded by a buffer zone of 148 km². This national park has some rare species of flora and fauna.

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is a lofty keyhole-shaped structure situated in the small village of Pallikkara, Kasaragod district spread over a total area of 40 acres.

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

It is a Hindu temple located in Thiruvananthapuram, the state capital of Kerala, India. It is considered as the richest place of worship in the world.

Ashtamudi Lake

.A gateway to the backwaters, Ashtamudi Lake is the most popular place in Kollam, which is surrounded by lush green trees and swaying coconut palms.

Kuruvadweep

Kuruvadweep or Kuruva Island is situated in Wayanad district of Kerala. It is a 950-acre (3.8 km2) protected river delta,ies on the banks of the tributaries of Kabini River

Willingdon Island

Willingdon Island is the largest artificial island in India, which forms part of the city of Kochi.It is a busy hub for commercial shipping and cruise liners.

Explore the Specialties of Kerala

Kerala is renowned for some of its unique cultural and geographical characteristics. The practices and traditions handed down from generation to generation, alongside some of the natural wonders that we have been blessed with have attracted people to this land for centuries. From the ancient healthcare system ofAyurveda to our picturesque hill stations and diverse wildlife, we provide you with links to access a wide range of our specialities. We believe it is our duty to share these gifts with the rest of the world.
